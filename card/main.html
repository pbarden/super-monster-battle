Introduction

What is Super Monster Battle?

"Super Monster Battle" is a product concept created by D Paul Barden to conicide with various course studies mixed with plain old fun.

The story so far...

"Super Monster Battle" takes place around the fictitious Aeon City. After single-handedly defeating an invading mob of Wokou, the Hero completes the trials of Mt. Horai and discovers a powerful force within.

The Hero can't waste much time perfecting the craft of battle before it's off to the grand battle tournament to claim the title of "Champion of Games."

Basics of Super Monster Battle

Super Monster Battle is a board game that uses card, token, and dice mechanics for gameplay between 2 and 6 players.

World of Monsters

The monsters depicted in this game are the work of AndHeGames, and D Paul Barden. Original artwork for these monsters can be downloaded here.

Different versions of the game

The base game also has 5 expansions available with game board extensions and exclusive, never-before-seen game cards.

The "Game Plus" expansion features Super Rare cards Red River Serpent, Radiant Behemoth, and Lambda Unchained. Other bonus expansions feature unique super rare cards and exclusive Ghost Rare cards based on the four legendary gods - Suzaku, Genbu, Seiryuu, and Byakko.

Starting a game

What you'll need:

  • Game board
  • Set of dice
  • Player tokens
  • 2-6 Players

When you set up the game board, each player may place their token on any open start space. Players take turns rolling dice to determine their play order - the player with the highest roll is Player 1, followed by the next highest as Player 2, and so on.

Each player starts with 5 gold and gets a bonus 1 gold at the beginning of each turn. On a player's first turn, that player draws 3 monster cards and chooses any common card among them to be added to that player's team.

Players take turns rolling the dice and moving around the board, following instructions for each space as a player may land on them. Each player will collect achievements along the way, later used to determine the winner of the game.
